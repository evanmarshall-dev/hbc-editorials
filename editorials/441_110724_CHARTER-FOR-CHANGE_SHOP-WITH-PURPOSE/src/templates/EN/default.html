Oshki Wupoowane | The Blanket Fund

Hudson's Bay Foundation is proud to partner with the Gord Downie & Chanie Wenjack Fund to create Oshki Wupoowane | The Blanket Fund.


Oshki Wupoowane launched on September 30th, 2022, with a commitment to direct 100% of net proceeds from the sale of all HBC Point Blankets to The Blanket Fund moving forward. Since then, The Blanket Fund has grown to more than $2 million, which will be disbursed to successful applicants over multiple years.

Hudson's Bay Foundation is a registered charity, working to address racial inequity by investing in three distinct pillars ‐ Education, Employment, and Empowerment. Through these pillars, the Foundation aims to increase opportunities for underrepresented communities across Canada.

In 2021, the Foundation launched Hudson's Bay Foundation Charter for Change, a 10-year, $30-million commitment to accelerate racial equity in Canada. To accomplish this, the Foundation formed long-term partnerships with organizations doing critical work and driving meaningful change. Together, the Foundation and its impact partners have reexamined the ways in which it invests in communities and the ways in which we can all create a better Canada.

Gord Downie & Chanie Wenjack Fund; Indspire; Future Generations Foundation; CEE Centre for Young Black Professionals; Nia Centre for the Arts; MLSE Foundation; The Centre for Addiction and Mental Health (CAMH); and Rainbow Railroad.

To learn about the programs we support visit: hudsonsbayfoundation.ca

Hudson's Bay Foundation is a registered charity.

Hudson's Bay's offices, stores and distribution centres are located on the traditional lands and unceded territories of First Nations, Métis and Inuit communities, which continue to be home to many Indigenous Peoples.

Hudson's Bay Company played a defining role in the colonization of Canada, and we are committed to listening to Knowledge Keepers and Elders, while being inspired by Indigenous youth, as we continue to form long-lasting relationships based on peace, friendship and respect with Indigenous communities through reflection, learning and action.

Acknowledging the land on which we operate is an important act in honouring and respecting the land that we all share as Indigenous and non-Indigenous Peoples.
